Reading about email spoofing is helpful because I learned how to protect myself. Spoofing is a string of emails sent from a fake sender address intending to look legitimate. People who are deceived by email spoofing are usually victims of financial loss and potential malware infecting their computer. Phishing scams are a form of email spoofing. I can apply this concept immediately to my environment by carefully vetting the emails received in my inbox before opening them, typically by reading the header preview, the sender address, or any grammatical errors. The article titled E-mail Overview describes basic elements in an email. There are a few basic elements, such as from, date, to, subject, CC, and BCC. CC stands for carbon copy and it is used when we want to keep recipients informed but they are not the intended recipients. BCC stands for Black carbon copy and it is when we do not want a recipient to know that someone else was added onto the message. CC can be a helpful element in my future workspace for sending out mass work emails intended for one person but visible to all employees, for example, such as a congratulatory Employee of the Month email. Researching Internet mailing lists and signing up for ones that interest me has been helpful. Internet mailing lists are lists of many email addresses that can be used to send email to multiple users simultaneously. One popular Internet mailing list is the Ticketmaster Verified Fan list. This is a mailing list intended to boost “legitimate” fans' chances of getting tickets to a concert by sending mass emails of special presale codes. I applied this concept to my current environment by signing up for the Verified Fan list for the Twice world tour which will significantly increase my opportunities at buying tickets for the world tour.
